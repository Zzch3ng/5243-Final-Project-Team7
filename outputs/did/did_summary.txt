NYC Congestion Pricing — DID Causal Analysis Summary

Policy launch date: 2025-01-05
Treatment group: 68 zones inside the Manhattan CBD
Control group: zones outside the CBD
Sample window: 2024-11-08 to 2025-02-28
Outcome: log(1 + n_trips) per zone-hour

Specification: log_trips ~ did + controls + zone FE + day FE
  controls: temp_c, precip_mm, is_rainy, is_weekend, is_holiday, hour-of-day FE
  Standard errors: clustered at zone level

MAIN RESULT (full specification):
  beta_DID: -0.2167
  Approximate effect: -19.48% in CBD demand post-launch (vs. non-CBD)
  95% CI: [-21.26%, -17.66%]
  p-value: 0.0000

ROBUSTNESS:
  - Pre-trend test: see event_study_coefficients.csv
  - Event-study plot: figures/event_study.png
  - Placebo policy date 4 weeks earlier: placebo_test.csv
  - Cluster heterogeneity: did_by_cluster.csv (if available)

INTERPRETATION:
  The DID coefficient measures the average change in CBD taxi demand after
  congestion pricing launched, relative to non-CBD zones over the same period.
  This nets out city-wide trends (weather, seasonality, broader demand shifts)
  that affect both treated and control zones.

  Identification rests on parallel trends — in the absence of the policy,
  CBD and non-CBD demand would have moved together. The pre-period leads
  in the event study and the placebo test address this assumption.

LIMITATIONS:
  - The CBD zone list is a static definition; actual fee enforcement may
    differ at zone borders.
  - DID estimates the average effect; cluster-level results show this is
    not uniform across zone types.
  - Yellow taxi data does not include for-hire vehicles, which may have
    been substituted in or out post-policy.
  - The post-period is only a few months; longer-run effects may differ.
