Interpretation for the congestion-pricing story:
Lag and rolling demand features dominate, which is expected for hourly demand prediction. Time-of-day and day-of-week features capture commuting and nightlife patterns. Spatial features (PULocationID, borough, zone_cluster from the unsupervised step) distinguish demand levels across the city. The post_policy flag captures a city-wide shift in demand around January 5, 2025; whether that shift is causally attributable to congestion pricing is examined separately in the DID analysis (06_did_analysis.ipynb), not in this predictive model.

Limitations:
- This is a predictive model, not a causal estimate of congestion pricing.
- Lag features are strong predictors and may dominate slower-moving policy or weather signals.
- Results depend on the quality of the zone-hour aggregation and missing-indicator design.
