NYC Taxi Demand — Stacking Ensemble Summary

Approach:
Combine 4 base models (Poisson, Ridge, RandomForest, LightGBM) into a stacking
ensemble. Out-of-fold predictions generated via TimeSeriesSplit (5 folds) form
the meta-learner's training data; the meta-learner is a Ridge regression with
non-negative weights.

Interpretation:
The Ridge meta-learner with non-negative constraint reveals which base models
the ensemble trusts most. Models with near-zero weights are effectively dropped
from the final prediction. The stacking improvement reflects the value of
combining model families with different inductive biases (count regression,
linear, tree ensemble, gradient boosting).

Limitations:
- Stacking adds ~30 minutes of training time vs. a single LightGBM model.
- The improvement margin is modest because base models share much of the same
  feature pipeline; ensemble diversity is limited.
- For the web app, we use the stacking ensemble for headline predictions and
  fall back to LightGBM alone for low-latency inference if needed.
